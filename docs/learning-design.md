## How Labs Work

Most exercises are done in pairs or small groups. Explaining your reasoning to a partner (not just the answer) is part of the work.

> [Feynman's learning technique](https://www.todoist.com/de/inspiration/feynman-technique): Explain it as if teaching someone new. Where you stumble is where the gap is.

Session usually have two modes:

- **Open exercises:** AI tools allowed, within the rules below.
- **AI-free zones:** Timed exercises, no AI tools, individual. These build the independent thinking you need.

## What You'll Practice

Exercises go beyond "describe what this code does." You'll trace data transformations, spot pipeline errors, and interpret metrics and plots. This will train your "analytical muscle" every session.

The [question catalogue](question-catalogue.md) lists all question types you'll encounter, organized by CRISP-DM phases: data understanding, preparation, modeling, evaluation, etc.

## Using AI in Labs

✅ AI use allowed · ⚠️ cautionary use only · ❌ AI use forbidden

### Key Rules

Rules to guard your capability to build your own understanding:
- **Attempt first.** Always try the question/problem yourself before asking AI.
- **Explain yourself.** Annotate and interpret code in your own words.
- **Reason yourself.** Write-ups must reflect your own understanding.
- **Never paste AI output as your answer.**

Also:
- **AI can be wrong.** Verify any AI output, even if they sound fluent.
- **AI-free zones: no AI.** No AI tools for AI-free exercises. No exceptions. 

### General AI Use

| | Practice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ | Explain code and concepts | Builds mental models without bypassing the thinking work |
| ✅ | Debug and interpret error messages | You decide what to implement and learn why the bug existed |
| ✅ | Suggest alternatives (after you attempt first) | Deepens your toolkit without replacing your initial thinking |
| ⚠️ | Brainstorming | Judge suggestions yourself. The first recommendation may not be the best |
| ⚠️ | Auto-completing or generating full solutions | Attempt first; shortcuts skip the struggle that builds mental models |
| ❌ | AI as author of write-ups | Write-ups must reflect your own understanding and reasoning |

### Code Reading Specific

| | Practice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ✅ | AI to explain code | One-shot for understanding new concepts is OK if the understanding sticks. |
| ⚠️ | AI summaries of data analysis | Bypasses deciding what matters and interpreting results yourself |
| ❌ | AI during AI-free zones | Builds independent thinking; mirrors the exam format |

> **AI-free zones** are short timed exercises with no AI tools. The goal is to gradually normalize working independently. This is the same condition you'll face in the exam.
